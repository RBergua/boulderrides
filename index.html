Boulder Group Rides

Every group ride you're looking for, all in one place! This site pulls upcoming rides from the most active cycling clubs in Boulder County and displays them on an interactive map.

This project is free and runs 24/7 on a personal server. If it's gotten you out on a ride, you can support it:

Every bit helps keep it alive!

Why this exists

Boulder's group ride scene is incredible. But finding out what's happening, when, and where usually means checking a dozen club pages. This project puts it all in one place.

A backend process pulls the latest events from the Strava and Ride with GPS API twice a day (12 PM & 1 AM) and filters them down to rides that begin near Boulder. The data is always updated and requires zero effort from the clubs themselves.

How to use it

Pick a day on the calendar. Highlighted days have rides or races/events, and the first available one loads automatically.

Tap a route on mobile or hover on desktop to see its details.

Solid lines are paved, dashed lines are unpaved (e.g., gravel). Each route has a pin that marks its start.

Checkered-flag pins mark upcoming races near Boulder. Tap the Races button to browse them, or click any checkered pin on the map to open the Races page with that event highlighted.

Use the Metric button to display the ride statistics in km and meters.

Mobile app

There is no need to download a separate app. This site adapts automatically to phones and tablets. Add it to your home screen for a full-screen, app-like experience.

On Android: open in Chrome, tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top right, and select "Add to Home screen".

On iPhone: open in Safari, tap the share button (↑) at the bottom, and select "Add to Home Screen".

Races & Events

Upcoming races and events around Boulder, CO within a 35-mile radius for the next 2 months. Whether you're racing, spectating, or volunteering, you'll find them here. Data is pulled weekly from the BikeReg API (Mondays at 3 AM).

Some major events (e.g., Triple Bypass, Mt. Blue Sky Hill Climb) are not listed in bikereg.com and are hardcoded in the backend process.
